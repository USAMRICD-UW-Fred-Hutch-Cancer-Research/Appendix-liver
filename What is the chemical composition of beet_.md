Beetroot (*Beta vulgaris* L.) is primarily composed of **water ($\\sim$88%), carbohydrates ($\\sim$10%), and fiber**, providing a low-calorie source of nutrients and bioactive compounds. Key components include high levels of inorganic nitrates (up to 1379 mg/kg), betalain pigments (betacyanins and betaxanthins) for antioxidant properties, and minerals like potassium, sodium, and magnesium. \[[1](https://www.mdpi.com/2077-0472/15/3/270), [2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beetroot), [3](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0308814618314146), [4](https://www.researchgate.net/figure/Proximate-composition-of-beetroot_tbl2_325057965#:~:text=A%20proximate%20analysis%20of%20beetroot%20\(Beta%20vulgaris,be%20found%20in%20Tables%202%20and%203.)\]

**Nutritional and Chemical Composition**

* **Proximate Analysis (Raw):** Approximately 87-88% moisture, 7.6% to 10% carbohydrates (largely sucrose), 1.5-2% protein, 0.3% fat, and 1.9% fiber.  
* **Pigments (Betalains):** Responsible for the deep red color, constituting 0.8 to 1.3 g/L in juice, consisting of red-violet betacyanins ($\\sim$60%) and yellow-orange betaxanthins ($\\sim$40%).  
* **Nitrates:** High levels of inorganic nitrate ($NO\_3^-$), which improve cardiovascular health and athletic performance.  
* **Minerals & Vitamins:** Rich in potassium, with lower amounts of calcium, iron, magnesium, phosphorus, and zinc. It also contains vitamins $B\_1$, $B\_2$, $B\_3$, $B\_5$, $B\_6$, $B\_9$ (folate), and $B\_{12}$, plus Vitamin C.  
* **Phytochemicals:** Contains polyphenols, flavonoids (rutin, quercetin), and saponins that provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant benefits. \[[1](https://www.mdpi.com/2077-0472/15/3/270), [2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beetroot), [3](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0308814618314146), [4](https://www.researchgate.net/figure/Proximate-composition-of-beetroot_tbl2_325057965#:~:text=A%20proximate%20analysis%20of%20beetroot%20\(Beta%20vulgaris,be%20found%20in%20Tables%202%20and%203.), [5](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0889157515001003), [6](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8229785/), [7](https://www.clinmedjournals.org/articles/jnmdc/journal-of-nutritional-medicine-and-diet-care-jnmdc-6-043.php?jid=jnmdc#:~:text=They%20have%20an%20important%20content%20of%20B%2Dvitamins,sesquiterpenoids%2C%20carotenoids%2C%20coumarins%2C%20flavonoids%20\(tiliroside%2C%20astragalin%2C%20rhamnocitrin%2C)\]

The composition makes beetroot highly effective for reducing blood pressure and supporting cardiovascular health due to the conversion of nitrate to nitric oxide in the body. \[[1](https://www.mdpi.com/2077-0472/15/3/270), [3](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0308814618314146), [8](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-031-80700-8_9), [9](https://austinpublishinggroup.com/nutrition-metabolism/fulltext/ajnm-v7-id1084.pdf#:~:text=It%20\(%20Red%20Beet%20\)%20is%20also,blood%2C%20oxygen%20and%20nutrients%20to%20the%20cells.), [10](https://www.facebook.com/BannerHealth/posts/beets-are-more-than-just-a-colorful-vegetable-theyre-rich-in-nutrients-and-natur/1379113497180738/#:~:text=Beetroot%2C%20also%20known%20as%20beets%2C%20is%20a,blood%20vessel%20relaxation%20and%20lowering%20blood%20pressure.)\]
